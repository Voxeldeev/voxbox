Frequently Asked Questions

What does Slarmoo's Box add?

Slarmoo's Box is a mod of Ultrabox, and has all of the same features that Ultrabox has. However, there are some cool additions to be aware of in Slarmoo's Box:
  • New envelopeable effects: reverb, chorus, bitcrush, frequency crush, panning, distortion, and arpeggio speed
  • Five new envelope types:
    • Pitch: Tracks what your notes do based on the pitch (also called keytracking in other daws). This envelope has an adjustable pitch range setting
    • Random: Uses a pseudorandom hashing function to output a "random" value based on the step size and seed. Each time your song is played the envelope will output the same value
    • Fall: Similar to twang, except instead of decreasing quickly to slowly, fall decreases slowly to quickly
    • Random: Produces a random value based on either the time or the pitch. This envelope has an adjustable seed (and steps for time) setting
    • LFO: Modulates a value based on a waveform. This envelope type replaced the old tremolo envelope
  • Individual envelope speeds (with modulation support), bounds, and inversions
  • Increased max envelope count to 16 (from ultrabox's 12)
  • New Song EQ filter, along with modulation support
  • The x128 and x256 fm frequencies
  • A better spectrum and harmonics editor (zoom, copy and paste)
  • Several new keybinds in the Song player (shorten link, toggle loop, toggle zoom, and copy link) and 2 new keybinds for opening and closing several dropdowns at once (envelopes and fm dropdowns)
  • Two new themes: Greyscale and Slarmoo's Box

Is Slarmoo's Box compatible with Ultrabox?

Yes, Slarmoo's Box will promperly import all songs from Ultrabox. Both changing the domain of the link and importing an Ultrabox json will work

What can I do with Pitch Envelopes?

Slarmoo's Box's unique pitch envelopes has a variety of unique uses. You can:
  • Set it to target pitch shift or detune (or custom unisons) to get anything from subtle pitch stretching to microtonality
  • Target note volume to have upper notes map quieter than lower notes or vice versa for help mixing
  • Target note volume to fade from one instrument to another (or suddenly change, which may be useful for multisampling)
  • Target pulse width, fm volume sliders, or other effects to have dynamic arpeggios
  • Target panning to have different notes be panned different directions
  • Have different pitches of a drumset or other instrument have different effect amounts
  • And many more techniques that you can discover on your own!
